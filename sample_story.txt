# The Boy Who Never Needed a Crowd
Some people collect friends like souvenirs.
Arjun never understood that.
He wasn't rude. He wasn't arrogant. He simply preferred silence over noise. While others moved in packs of ten or fifteen, Arjun was content with one close friend. Two, at most. Conversations exhausted him. Small talk felt like solving a puzzle with no reward. Especially with girls, he never knew what to say. So he usually said nothing.
His classmates called him "the quiet one."
He didn't mind.
One ordinary afternoon, while walking through the college corridor, someone suddenly blocked his path.
A girl stood there with a Dairy Milk chocolate.
"You want this?" she asked with a teasing smile.
Arjun looked at the chocolate.
Then at her.
He nodded slightly.
Just as he reached for it, she quickly pulled it back.
"Nope!"
She laughed and walked away.
For the first time in a long while, someone had forced a smile onto his face.
The next day she repeated the same joke.
Then again.
Soon it became their strange little tradition.
She would tease him.
He would pretend not to care.
She would laugh.
Without realizing it, the boy who barely spoke to anyone had started waiting for those tiny moments every day.
Weeks passed.
One weekend, Arjun planned to spend the evening at a nearby park with his closest friend.
Simple plan.
Just the two of them.
When he arrived, his friend was already there.
Not alone.
Three girls stood beside him.
Arjun quietly sighed.
"This wasn't the plan."
His friend grinned.
"Relax. They're nice."
Arjun wanted to leave.
But before he could, one of the girls walked over.
"Hi. You're Arjun, right?"
He nodded.
"I'm Nila."
That was all.
A simple introduction
Nothing dramatic.
Nothing cinematic.
Yet somehow, it became the first page of a story neither of them expected.
Nila talked enough for both of them.
She asked questions
He answered with short sentences
She laughed at his awkward replies.
He slowly laughed with her.
Days became weeks.
Weeks became months.
Late-night chats replaced silent evenings.
Morning texts became routine.
They walked through parks.
Shared ice creams.
Argued over movies.
Took blurry selfies.
Watched sunsets without saying much.
She learned that silence wasn't emptiness.
It was simply the language he spoke best.
For the first time in his life, Arjun didn't have to pretend to be more talkative.
She accepted the quiet version of him.
And that made him fall in love.
He never planned anything.
He never calculated the risks.
He never asked himself what would happen later.
Every decision came from his heart
Every moment felt right.
So he loved without holding back.
He trusted without keeping a backup plan.
He gave everything
Then...
One day, the conversations became shorter.
Replies took hours.
Then days.
Calls stopped.
The "good mornings" disappeared.
The park where they once spent evenings became just another place.
No fights.
No dramatic goodbye.
Sometimes love doesn't end with an explosion.
Sometimes it fades quietly, like the last colors of sunset disappearing into the night.
Eventually...
Everything finished.
The photos remained.
The memories remained.
The places remained.
Only the relationship didn't.
Months later, people still saw Arjun walking alone.
Earphones in.
Hands inside his pockets.
Watching the world pass by.
He had become the same quiet boy everyone once knew.
The same boy who never wanted a crowd.
The same boy with only one or two friends.
Only this time...
His silence carried memories.
Sometimes he would pass a chocolate shop and remember the girl who teased him with a chocolate.
Sometimes he'd visit the same park and smile for reasons no one else could understand.
People assumed he had moved on because he never spoke about her.
But some stories are never told out loud.
They stay folded inside the heart, like an old letter whose ink has faded but whose meaning never does.
He didn't regret loving her.
For a while, she had turned his ordinary days into something beautiful.
And even though the ending wasn't the one he dreamed of, she had taught him something important.
Even the quietest hearts can love the loudest.
So he kept walking.
Not because he had forgotten.
But because life never waits for anyone.
Maybe one day someone else would stop him on an ordinary afternoon.
Maybe she'd hold out a chocolate with a playful smile.
Or maybe not.
Until then...
The quiet boy kept roaming alone, carrying a love story that had ended, but would always remain one of the happiest chapters of his life.
